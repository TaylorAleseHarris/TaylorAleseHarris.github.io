FAQ's

What colored fungi should I be most cautious of?

Red..

What makes fungi different from other plants?

They reproduce from spores, unlike plants that reproduce from seeds. Also unlike plants, fungi do not produce their own food and have to obtain both food and water from other sources.

Do fungi grow everywhere in the world?

Yes! They grow on all the continents, a few even in Antarctica.

What's so special about fungi?

  • Without the various types of fungi on Earth, we would soon be knee-deep in dead leaves and other plant matter that would refuse to rot.

  • Trees that died would continue to lie where they fell. Only fungi can break down lignin, the tough material at the heart of woody tissue.

  • Our crops would fail because the nutrients that fungi recycle from dead plant tissue would no longer be available.

  • Important farm animals like sheep would be unable to digest grass without the help they get from some friendly fungi.

What can fungi be used for?

Humans use fungi for many purposes, including as food or in the preparation of food. Humans also use fungi for pest control. In addition, fungi can be used to produce citric acid, antibiotics, and human hormones.
